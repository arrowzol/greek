position:
    Attributive Adj:
        <article><n><article><adj>
            = the elephant the big
            restrictive use of <article>
            Rather than merely ascribing a quality to the noun,
                this form gives a little more emphasis
                    to that quality represented by the adjective
                and helps to set this noun apart
                    from other ones without this quality
        <article><adj><n>
            = the big elephant
        <adj><n>
            = big elephant
        <n><adj>
            = elephant big

    Predicative Adj:
        -- The adjective itself is actually making the statement about the noun
        <article><n><to be><adj>.
            = The elephant is big.
        <adj><to be><article><n>.
            = Big is the elephant.
    Predicative Adj with omission of <to be>:
        <adj><article><n>.
            = Big the elephant.
        <article><n><adj>.
            = The elephant big.

    Substantive Adj (takes the place of a noun):
        <article><adj>
            = the big
